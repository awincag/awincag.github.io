Philosophy & Process
"Simple ain't Easy" — Thelonious Monk

Designing simple and elegant solutions are 1) rarely discovered the first 25+ tries 2) not simple at all. I firmly believe that designing a beautiful user experience and a great product, boils down to a lot grinding and a whole lot of iteration (Joe Stewart of Work & Co, would call it 'brute force').

Great design all starts with people problems. Without a problem, you have a feature, not a digital product or service. A truly great product doesn't just provide more than just a fantastic flow, amicable animations and eye-candy branding (it definitely needs all of that), but it needs to provide value and utility to the user.

Finding and solving these problems starts with understanding people and businesses, not just how to code. Being able to optimize a stylesheet is great for improving the 0.5 seconds of a persons life, but if that product doesn't solve their problem, you just wasted a lot more than 0.5 seconds. To this end, with all my projects, I truly push to find that deeper problem that people have and solve it, really, really well. It's not the "what lawnmower do I use?" It's the "what's the best way to key my grass short."
